Bttrme — interaction design proposal

Gate 6 deliverable · self-contained · realistic sample data · click anything that looks tappable

This prototype shows the twelve required flows as phone-first screens (plus one desktop view for weekly planning). It encodes the approved principles from Phases 1–4:

  • Front-loaded decisions. Every screen the user meets at a heavy moment presents pre-decided context and bounded choices — never open questions.
  • Non-judgmental vocabulary, structurally. No red anywhere in the palette. No percentages, streaks, or "overdue." States are completed / not completed / deferred / moved. Grammar rule: facts belong to blocks and weeks, never to "you."
  • Honesty is cheap. Skip is one tap with an undo — cheaper than pretending. Recovery from a missed afternoon is two taps.
  • Calm surfaces. Warm neutrals, one teal accent, soft category colors on the block's left edge (work · family · personal · health · rest).
Interactive bits to try: the execution screen's full Start → Done → observation flow and its "time passed" recovery simulation; placing pool blocks in daily planning; closing the day; carry / rephrase / let go on goals; dismissing an insight; regenerating the calendar token.

1 · Login

PRD 01 — the only unauthenticated screen
Bttrme
From intention to action.
Private, single-person app. Sessions last ~30 days.

Why it looks like this

  • No sign-up, no "forgot password" link — a single-user tool shouldn't pretend to be a service. Resets happen out of band.
  • The tagline states the product's one job. No feature tour, no onboarding carousel: the app teaches itself one calm screen at a time.
  • After a rate-limit lockout the message is "Try again in a little while" — plain, no alarm styling.
Tap Sign in to land on the weekly overview — the returning-user default is Today, but the first visit of a week lands here.

Why it looks like this

  • Each template already answers the execution moment's questions: outcome and first physical action are required fields, written once, at a calm moment (PRD 02 FR-1).

Why it looks like this

  • The day is three shelves, not 24 slots. Parts of day are the default granularity (Phase 1 hypothesis); an exact time like "9:00" is an optional extra on the block, not a grid you must fill.

Why it looks like this

  • One sheet holds both what (copied from the template, freely editable per-instance) and when — dates, part of day, optional exact time (Phase 5 requirement: "clear editing of dates, times, duration, expected outcome").
  • The outcome field's label teaches its own philosophy: "what counts as done." The sample text — "any session counts" — is the anti-perfectionism principle in the data itself.

Why it looks like this

  • The card answers every question the negotiating mind will raise — what, how long, why today, what counts as done — and makes the first action the visually loudest element. The bar to clear is "put on shoes," not "gym."

8 · End-of-day review

PRD 04 — derived, one tap, under a minute, skippable without trace
BttrmeClosing Monday

TodayWeekLibraryReview

Why it looks like this

  • Everything above the note field is derived — the user confirms a picture, he doesn't fill out a form. One tap plus optional one line keeps it under 60 seconds.
  • "Moved to Tuesday" and "not completed" wear the same quiet grey chip as each other. Auto-closed blocks and chosen skips are phrased identically — the system never distinguishes "gave up" from "chose not to."
  • The skip affordance is written into the screen itself: "just close the app." No streak exists to protect.
  • The closing line counts facts, never ratios — "4 completed · 1 moved," not "67%."
Tap Close the day.

Why it looks like this

  • Three verbs, none judgmental: carry / rephrase / let go. "Let go" is a decision, not an admission — there is no "failed" path out of a goal.
  • Lineage is visible but gentle: "week 2 of this goal." Whether long lineage deserves an insight ("smaller blocks?") is decided in the insights module, never here.
  • The cap is the interface again: slots, not warnings. Carrying two + rephrasing one leaves exactly one free slot.
  • The planning note rides along in its own card — last week's learning physically enters next week's planning screen (PRD 04 FR-12).
Try it: switch a goal between carry / rephrase / let go.

11 · Insights

PRD 05 — the mirror: sentences, not charts; reachable only from weekly planning
BttrmeBefore you plan
From your last 4 weeks. Patterns belong to blocks and weeks — never to you.
Blocks of 30 minutes or less were started far more often than 90-minute blocks. Splitting long blocks may help.
Evening blocks were moved or not completed in 3 of the last 4 weeks. Consider planning lighter evenings.
Gym blocks were followed by “energy: better” in 5 of 6 observations.
Do-nothing breaks were marked restorative 4 times out of 5. Evening scrolling was not marked restorative once.
“Send the project proposal draft” is in its second week. The completed blocks for it were the morning ones.
TodayWeekLibraryReview

Why it looks like this

  • Five hand-written sentence templates filled with the user's parameters — no charts, because a chart invites score-reading; no free-form generated text, because phrasing is a safety surface here.
  • Every sentence pairs an observation with an optional planning suggestion, and ends pointing forward: "Plan the week →". The mirror exists to improve the next plan, not to grade the last one.
  • "Not useful" suppresses that template for a month — the user curates his own mirror (PRD 05 FR-8). All-dismissed leaves a quiet empty panel, not a plea for engagement.
  • No badge, no unread count, no home-screen entry. If he never taps into this screen, it never follows him.
Try it: dismiss an insight.

12 · Calendar export

PRD 06 — a private ICS feed into the calendar he already lives in
BttrmeSettings · Calendar

Regenerating kills the old link instantly. Anyone with the old URL sees nothing.

What the calendar shows

First actions and notes never leave Bttrme. Completion states are never exported — the calendar sees the plan, not the record. Rest blocks are included.

Connect

Apple Calendar — Settings → Add subscription → paste the link.
Google Calendar — Other calendars → From URL → paste the link.
Google refreshes subscribed feeds on its own schedule — sometimes hours. Your phone's calendar updates faster.

Your data

Last automatic backup: tonight, 03:12 ok
TodayWeekLibraryReview

Why it looks like this

  • Subscription, not sync: no OAuth, no permissions dialogs, no second source of truth. The feed shows the plan forward — completed/missed states stay home (PRD 06 FR-3/FR-4).
  • The privacy model is explained in one breath where the choice is made: names only by default; first actions never exported; regeneration as the leak response.
  • The refresh-lag caveat is stated at setup, in plain words, so a slow Google refresh reads as "Google," not "broken."
  • Backup status lives here too — the one place the app is allowed to warn is about its own care of your data.
Try it: regenerate the feed token.
